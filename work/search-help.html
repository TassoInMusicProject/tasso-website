Search help

For text searches, type any full or partial word to highlight words in the score. Sequences of words are not currently allowed. Text searching is independent from music searching. For music searches, please follow the instructions below. Note that music searches can be combined with one another (i.e., you can search by pitch, interval, and rhythm at the same time in any combination).

Melodic searching

Pitch a  through  g Diatonic pitch names (any chromatic inflection).
n, #, - Optional chromatic quality (natural, sharp, flat).
r Rest.
Interval 1 Repeated note (but can have a chromatic alteration).
P1 Repeated note, or perfect unison (with same accidental).
2, -2 Up a second or down a second (can be major, minor, augmented, or diminished).
M2, -M2 Up a second or down a major second.
3, -3 Up third or down a third (can be major, minor, augmented or diminished)..
m3, -m3 Up third or down a minor third.
P4, -A4 Up a perfect fourth, down an augmented fourth.
P5, -d5 Up a perfect fifth, down a diminished fifth.
8, -8 Up or down an octave.
Rhythm    0, 1, 2, 4, 8 breve, whole, half, quarter, eighth (both pitches and rests).
. augmentation dot (e.g., 2. for a dotted half note)

Sample melodic searches

cdefg (in pitch search box) Diatonic pitch classes C, D, E, F, and G in sequence with any duration. (spaces between notes are optional). This will also match to C, D, E-flat, F, G, since it will allow any chromatic inflections.
cdefg (in interval search box) This search will be treated as the diatonic interval search "2222".
cndne-fngn (in pitch search box) Search for C-natural, D-natural, E-flat, F-natural, and G-natural in sequence.
cndne-fngn (in interval search box) This search will convert automatically to the chromatic interval search "M2m2M2M2".
2-31 (in interval search box) Up a second, down a third, then a repeated pitch (with any chromatic inflections).
M2-m3P1 (in interval search box) Up a major second, down a minor third, then a repeated pitch (with specific chromatic inflections).
1442 (in rhythm search box) Whole note (or rest), two quarter notes (or rests), then a half note (or rest).
ceg / 33 (in pitch / interval search boxes) Search for C, E, and G in the pitch search box. Enter "33" in the interval search box to require the C-E interval to be a third (instead of a sixth) and E-G to be a third (instead of a sixth).

Harmonic searching

Pitch (ace) Parentheses indicate pitches that must sound at the same time.
(=ace) Adding an equals sign requires that only the given pitches be present in the vertical sonority.
(==acce) Adding two equals signs requires an exact number of pitches in the vertical sonority. In this example, the sonority must have one A, two C's, and one E.

Mixed harmonic and melodic searching

Pitch g(ace)b Search for the melodic sequence G, A, B, with the pitches C and E occuring at the same time as the A in other voices.
