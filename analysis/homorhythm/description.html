Description The following table lists homorhythmic texture percentages found in each work. Identification of homorhythm is done with an algorithm, so measurements are approximate. The algorithm to identify homorhythmic regions in the music works as follows:
  • First identify sonorities containing three or more simultaneous note attacks. These sonorities are assigned a score of 1.
  • Then assign a score of 1/2 to any sonority sandwiched between two sonorities with a score of 1.
  • Then search for contiguous regions of music with non-zero scores that exceed a total score threshold, which for this analysis was set to 4 (at least three successive attack sonorities with up to one low-attack sonority between them).
To view the homorhythmic textures in the musical notation, click on the percentage for a work. Textures are highlighted in red and green: red indicates a high-attack region, and green indicates a low-attack region between two high-attack regions. Raw data for further analysis can be found on the spreadsheet: http://bit.ly/tasso-homorhythm.
