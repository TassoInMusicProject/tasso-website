COMMENTARY

This three-voice piece by Roman composer Giovannelli appeared in the second book of the anthology Fiori musicali, assembled by Giacomo Vincenzi and dedicated to Francesco Sugana of Treviso. Like the first book of the anthology, it primarily features works by Roman and Venetian composers, many based on texts by Tasso. Giovannelli’s three-voice setting of “Non è questa la mano” is striking not only for its liveliness — note the hectic repetition of rhythmic-melodic motives in {{mm 30 33}} — but also for its ingenuous word painting. In particular, Giovannelli gives a musical representation of the poem’s subject, the female hand, by referencing the Guidonian hand at the beginning and at the end of the setting – note the hexachordum durum G-E in {{mm 1 2}} and {{mmx 5 6}} in C and B, respectively, and the hexachordum naturale C-A in {{mm 2 4}} (ascending) and {{mm 30 31}} (descending) in the T and B, respectively. Giovannelli may have borrowed this idea from Marenzio, whose 1581 setting of the same poem also features hexachords (see {{work "Trm0047b"}}).
